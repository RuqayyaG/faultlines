Services inflation is stuck at 5.7%. The housing market is softening faster than the OBR projected in March. And Andrew Bailey is sitting on his hands.

The MPC has a problem that doesn't resolve cleanly in either direction. Cut rates and you signal that inflation is beaten before it actually is — services CPI hasn't moved meaningfully in four months. Hold and you watch mortgage approvals fall for a sixth consecutive month while business investment quietly contracts.

Sterling is trading like none of this matters. That's the interesting part.

## What the market is missing

GBP/USD has held relatively steady despite the deteriorating domestic picture. The consensus view seems to be that the Bank will cut once in Q3 and the economy muddles through. That might be right. But it's a remarkably sanguine read of a central bank that has been consistently behind the curve since 2021.

The risk isn't that the MPC cuts too early. It's that they hold too long, something breaks in the housing market, and they're forced into an aggressive cutting cycle that spooks the gilt market.

That scenario isn't priced in. It should at least be on the table.
